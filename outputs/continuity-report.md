### CONFLICT #1 — Melpomene's Post-Shift Insult: Minor Wording Variation
**Severity:** Low
**File:** Prequel Part 2, Chapter 5: The Bar

**What the series bible says:**
> *"The service was different and not consistent with how we run things here. These are my creations. They cannot be altered. If you want to run a faulty kitchen, then you should start your own."*

**What the prequel says:**
> "The service was different tonight. Not consistent with how we run things here."
> "These are my creations. My menu. My techniques. They cannot be altered because a piece of equipment decides to malfunction. You don't have the authority to restructure my floor."
> "If you want to run a faulty kitchen, Vieux, then you should start your own."

**What Book 1 says:**
Book 1 does not reproduce the insult verbatim — it is described in retrospect (Ch. 6, The Star Card: *"the executive chef's menu while his own original concepts burned a hole in his pocket"*) rather than quoted. The insult is recapped by Baron in Chapter 6 of the prequel when he tells Calliope: *"That if I want to run a faulty kitchen, I should start my own."*

**Assessment:** The prequel's version is an expanded, dramatized rendering of the core insult. The spirit, the key phrases, and the catalyst function are all preserved. The added lines ("My menu. My techniques." / "You don't have the authority to restructure my floor.") add dramatic texture but do not contradict the series bible.

**Recommended Resolution:** No change required. If the author wants the series bible's exact wording to be canonical for future reference (e.g., if a later book quotes the insult), update the series bible to reflect the prequel's final dramatized version rather than the compressed summary currently in the bible.

### CONFLICT #3 — Gulf Remedy Described as a "Spice Blend" in Book 1
**Severity:** Low-Medium
**File:** Published Book 1, Chapter 1: The Palate of Fate

**What Book 1 says (Ch. 1):**
> *"Inside one, he was emulsifying the base for 'The Gulf Remedy,' a restorative, complex blend of spices that captured the deep, soulful essence of the coast."*

**What Book 1 says (Ch. 2) and the series bible say:**
The Gulf Remedy is a composed dish — chunky guacamole with pickled jalapeño brine, 8oz Jumbo Lump Crabmeat dressed with olive oil, lime, Creole seasoning, and Peychaud's Bitters, finished with fanned radish, micro-cilantro, smoked paprika, and plantain chips. It is plated in a chilled mortar bowl. It is explicitly a starter dish, not a "spice blend."

**What the prequel says:**
The prequel does not attempt a full description of the Gulf Remedy in the extracted chapters, so it does not directly contradict. However, Book 1's own Chapter 1 contradicts Book 1's Chapter 2 on this point.

**Assessment:** This is an internal inconsistency within Book 1 itself, not a prequel-vs.-published-book conflict. Chapter 1 of Book 1 was likely drafted before the Gulf Remedy was fully conceptualized as a composed dish. The description "blend of spices" being emulsified in a blender does not match the chunky, unblended, cold-assembled dish described in Chapter 2.

**This is flagged for the author's awareness.** The prequel should not repeat the "blend of spices" language from Book 1 Ch. 1 when describing the Gulf Remedy — it should be consistent with the more detailed, accurate description in Book 1 Ch. 2 and the series bible.

**Recommended Resolution:** If a revised edition of Book 1 is ever produced, the Chapter 1 description should be updated to match the fully realized dish. For the prequel, when the Gulf Remedy is described or referenced, use the series bible definition as the authority (not Book 1 Ch. 1).

### CONFLICT #4 — Scarlet Elixir Described as "Built on the Gulf Remedy" in Book 1
**Severity:** Medium
**File:** Published Book 1, Chapter 1: The Palate of Fate

**What Book 1 says (Ch. 1):**
> *"The glaze is my own design… I call it the Scarlet Elixir. It's a reduction built on the Gulf Remedy—a spice blend that pulls the deep water and the coastal heat right to the surface."*

**What the series bible says:**
The Scarlet Elixir's build is: Roasted tomato + smoked guajillo + Worcestershire + Peychaud's Bitters + apple cider vinegar + dark roux + cane sugar + white pepper + cayenne. The Gulf Remedy is a separate composed dish (guacamole + crabmeat + garnish). The Scarlet Elixir is not built on the Gulf Remedy — they share some flavor DNA (coastal heat, Peychaud's Bitters) but are distinct creations.

**What the prequel says (Ch. 7):**
The prequel gives the Scarlet Elixir a fully independent origin story — Baron makes it in his apartment from scratch, from the ingredients listed in the series bible. There is no mention of the Gulf Remedy as a foundation for the Scarlet Elixir. The two are treated as separate creations.

**Assessment:** Book 1 Ch. 1 describes the Scarlet Elixir as being "built on the Gulf Remedy." The prequel and the series bible treat them as distinct, independently developed creations. This is a conflict between the published book and the prequel.

The published book is the authority, so the prequel technically needs to align with it — or the author needs to decide whether Baron's description to Olivia in Book 1 was loose/poetic language (meaning "built on the same coastal philosophy as the Gulf Remedy") rather than a literal claim that the Elixir is made from the Remedy's base.

**Recommended Resolution — two options:**
1. **Insert a bridge line in the prequel** (Ch. 7 or a later chapter) where Baron acknowledges that the Scarlet Elixir draws on the same foundational flavor philosophy as the Gulf Remedy — coastal heat, Peychaud's Bitters, acid — even if they are technically separate preparations. This preserves both the prequel's origin story and Book 1's description.
2. **Accept that Book 1 Ch. 1's language is imprecise.** Baron is speaking to Olivia for the first time; "built on the Gulf Remedy" could read as "built on the same philosophy" rather than a literal recipe derivation. No change required to the prequel — but the author should be aware of the potential reader confusion.

This is flagged for the author to decide. Do not change the prequel without the author's explicit direction.
