# Apothecary Family Meal — Individual Character Posts
## Instagram, quote-card style — six standalone posts
### Created: 2026-07-24

**Context:** This is the opposite of `outputs/apothecary-crew-carousel-post-copy-v1.md`, which put the whole crew into one carousel. Here, each Apothecary crew member — plus Baron himself — gets their own standalone post, not a swipe sequence.

**Shared scene (not in the manuscript as written — an original angle built for social, consistent with the series bible's Chapter 1 "Heartbeat" crew and the found-family theme):** Preshift family meal. Before the rush hits, the crew gathers to eat together — a real restaurant-industry tradition. Baron has made red beans and rice, the classic humble New Orleans Monday dish, for everyone. It's the deliberate contrast to the high-stakes plated food the same hands will send out all night. Each post is written from that one character's angle, tied to what the series bible already establishes about them (Rampart's fatherhood and quiet-authority arc, Slide's sauce-pan ribbing, Dex's risotto clash with Rampart, Farrah's uncredited coverage, Ghost's dish pit). Same scene, six different lenses.

**Spoiler policy:** No plot spoilers. This is atmosphere and character, not story beats from the manuscript's actual chapters.

## Posting notes

- These six are built to run as a standalone sequence over consecutive days (not a single-day carousel) — post Baron first or last as the anchor, with the five crew posts filling the days between. Anchoring with Baron on day one (establishing the meal/scene) and closing with Baron or Rampart (the two most "leadership-voiced" posts) bookends the set well.
- Each post can be paired with its named image file directly — no new image generation needed for this batch.
- Red beans and rice is doing real narrative work here: it's the dish nobody puts on a menu, which is exactly why it works as the found-family symbol against the high-stakes plated food (Scarlet Redfish, Gulf Remedy) the same crew produces under pressure all night. Keep that contrast in any caption edits — humble food, high-stakes room.
- If cross-posting to Facebook, these can be lightly expanded (2-3 sentences of scene-setting added before the quote) to match that platform's warmer, longer-form tone — see the existing Facebook launch pack referenced in `outputs/instagram-baron-vieux-page-launch-v1.md` for the pairing convention.
