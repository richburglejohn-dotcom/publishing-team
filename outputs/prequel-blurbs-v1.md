## Long Blurb — Amazon KDP Description (250 words)

Before Bayouboujee, there was a gravel lot off Magazine Street and a man who had already outlasted everything the city could throw at him.

Baron Vieux has worked every room that matters in New Orleans — Kaldi's on Decatur at nineteen, reading strangers before they reached the counter. Sales floors from here to South Texas, where he was the best rep in the building. A general manager's office where he cut inventory costs by forty percent and still couldn't own a single shelf. Then COVID hit, and the professional world went remote, and Baron did something most men with his resume would never do: he applied for a line cook position and started over from scratch.

That decision leads him to ACE Hotel, to Peche, to the punishing silence of a Triple-A Five Diamond kitchen at the Four Seasons, and finally to The Apothecary — where he runs the line for a man who resents what Baron is.

The night Executive Chef Melpomene tells him *if you want to run a faulty kitchen, you should start your own* is the night Bayouboujee stops being a blueprint and becomes a reckoning.

But first: a storage room ritual and a crew worth protecting. A Tuesday night alone in his apartment where a reduction of roasted tomato, smoked guajillo, and Peychaud's Bitters becomes the Scarlet Elixir — his signature, never written down, belonging to no one but him. A walk home past a voice he doesn't yet recognize. And a city that has been arranging things all along.

*The Scarlet Foundation* is the origin story of a man who learned that ownership is not handed down. It is built, one shift at a time, on your own terms.

## Medium Blurb — Goodreads (150 words)

Baron Vieux didn't walk into a kitchen because it was his passion. He walked in because he was done executing other people's visions.

By the time he takes the sous chef position at The Apothecary, he has already been through Kaldi's Coffee, corporate sales, general management, and a pandemic that erased the career he'd built. He starts from scratch at line cook. He works up. He builds the Scarlet Elixir on a Tuesday off-shift from memory, and he never writes it down, because some things belong only to the person who made them.

When Executive Chef Melpomene delivers the insult meant to diminish him, it does the opposite. The LLC is already filed. The domain is already live. The city has already been making its arrangements.

*The Scarlet Foundation* moves the way New Orleans moves — heavy with history, alive with heat, and building toward something no one can take from you.

## Short Blurb — Social / Pitch (75 words)

Baron Vieux has run corporate sales floors, managed restaurants, and survived a pandemic that erased everything he built. Now he's sous chef at someone else's kitchen, executing someone else's vision, while a signature reduction simmers on his own stove at home.

One insult from the wrong man on the wrong night changes the math.

*The Scarlet Foundation* is how Bayouboujee gets born. New Orleans as witness. Ownership as the only ending that matters.

## Series Hook — Connecting Prequel to Book 1

Baron Vieux ends *The Scarlet Foundation* with an LLC, a blueprint, and a voice he heard once off a side street — a voice that said something offhand and disappeared into the French Quarter dark.

*A Symphony of Love in the Big Easy* begins where the city's arrangements finally pay off: the moment Olivia walks into Bayouboujee, and Baron realizes the kitchen wasn't the only thing he's been building toward.

Two equals. One restaurant. Everything on the line — and both of them know it.
