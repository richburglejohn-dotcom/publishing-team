# Author's Note — On Kaldi's

*Draft for inclusion in The Scarlet Foundation (and/or the Author's Note section of the completed Kaldi's Series). Place at the front or back of the manuscript — front matter is more common for context readers need going in; back matter works if you want the story to land first.*

---

**AUTHOR'S NOTE**

Kaldi's Coffee House was a real place — 941 Decatur Street, in the heart of the French Quarter, open from 1990 until 2000. For those of us who spent time there, it wasn't just a coffee shop. It was a living room for the Quarter's artists, musicians, students, and wanderers, and for a lot of us, it was where we became who we were going to be.

Baron Vieux's time behind that counter is fiction. The Baron Vieux you'll meet in these pages is a character, and the events of his life at Kaldi's are imagined. But the place itself — the espresso machine, the Venetian Crème, the forty-foot ceilings, the sense that you'd found somewhere you belonged — that part is true, and it's written with nothing but love for what Kaldi's was and what it meant to the people who walked through its doors.

This book is a tribute to that place and to everyone who remembers it the way I do. If you were there, I hope you feel it in these pages.

— Lejohn Richburg III

# The Apothecary — Written Consent Request & Author's Note

## 1. Consent Request (email or letter to Marita Crandle)

*Purpose: turn the verbal "I'm happy for you" into something on record. Keep it warm — she's already supportive — this is just making sure that support is protected in writing for both of you.*

---

Subject: A quick favor — putting our conversation in writing

Marita,

I wanted to follow up on something and put it in writing, mostly for my own peace of mind as the books move forward.

When A Symphony of Love in the Big Easy came out, I used The Apothecary's name and drew on my time working there — including some inspiration from you as an owner and fellow author — without asking beforehand. You were kind enough to tell me you were happy about it, and I don't want to just leave that as a conversation we had once.

Would you be comfortable confirming in writing (even just a reply to this email) that you're okay with:

1. The Apothecary's name being used in the Culinary Crescendo series (published and any future books), and
2. The character inspired in part by you appearing in the story, fictionalized as it is?

If there's anything you'd want changed, handled differently, or credited a certain way, I'm glad to talk it through — this series means a lot to me and I want it to represent The Apothecary and you fairly and with respect.

Thank you for everything — including inspiring me to start writing in the first place.

— Lejohn

## 2. Author's Note — On The Apothecary

*Use once written consent is received. Pairs with the Kaldi's note above — can run as one combined Author's Note section, or as two short separate notes.*

---

**AUTHOR'S NOTE**

The Apothecary is a real bar in New Orleans, and much of what happens within these pages is drawn from my own year and a half working there. Its owner, Marita Crandle — an author in her own right — was kind enough to welcome this story once she learned it existed, and her support means more to me than she probably knows.

The characters and events depicted are fictionalized, and any resemblance to real staff, regulars, or nights on that floor is shaped by memory and imagination working together. But the spirit of the place — and the fact that working there is part of why I started writing at all — is entirely true.

— Lejohn Richburg III
